Overview

Workshop Details

Welcome to the IROS 2025 Workshop on 3D World Generation for Robots and Autonmous Sytems!


The RoboGen workshop aims to explore cutting-edge neural 3D world generation techniques and their applications in robotics and autonomous systems. By bringing together researchers from computer vision, graphics, and robotics, we seek to bridge the gap between state-of-the-art neural fields, Gaussian splatting, and generative models and their practical deployment in robot learning tasks.
The workshop also seeks to address challenges in simulating the 3D physical world, and democratizing access to high-quality datasets - which will in turn be helpful in developing and robustifying AI/ML systems across various robotics domains.

Topics in Focus


Through invited keynote talks, poster-presentations and discussions, the workshop will address several key topics:
  • The advantages of NeRFs and 3DGS beyond reconstruction and rendering for the robotics context
  • Practical aspects of training and testing of neural methods, and the speed/efficiency trade-offs
  • Integration of 3D World representation methods (like NeRFs and 3D-Gasusians) with generative models
  • New applications of generative models in robot planning, perception and SLAM


Workshop Features


The workshop will feature the following events and activities to encourage discussion and participation on these topics:
  • Industry-Academia Keynote Sessions
    Leading researchers and practitioners from both academia and industry will present cutting-edge advancements in neural rendering and 3D generative models for robotics applications.
  • Oral Presentations
    Selected paper presentations highlighting impactful approaches to data generation, artifact mitigation, and domain-specific applications of neural rendering and generative models for robotics.
  • Interactive Poster & Demo Session
    A dedicated session where student researchers can demonstrate their methods and techniques, fostering discussions and collaborations. This will provide an opportunity for junior researchers to directly interact with senior researchers.
  • Expert Panel Discussion
    A focused discussion on bridging the gap between theoretical advancements in neural rendering and practical robotics applications.
  • Mentor 1:1 Session
    Beyond formal talks and breaks, we're facilitating personalized 15-30 minute one-on-one sessions between junior researchers and keynote speakers or organizing committee members during IROS. Prior to the conference, RoboGen workshop participants will receive a signup form to indicate their interests. This creates valuable mentorship opportunities, connecting junior researchers with senior experts for both technical guidance and career development.
  • Award Ceremony
    Different awards for best oral, most Impactful open-source project, and most engaging speaker are designed to encourage more participation.




Paper Submission

Call for Papers

📚 Topics



The RoboGen workshop will accept papers in relation to the application of Neural Fields and 3D Gaussian Splatting to robotics areas. Topics of this workshop include but are not limited to:

  • Real-time 3D Gaussian Splatting for embodied robot learning and navigation
  • Sim-to-real transfer using neural 3D world generation for robotic policy learning
  • Neural rendering for perception in adverse weather conditions (low-light, bad weather, extreme long range, very sparse view, occlusions)
  • Language-guided 3D scene generation for robot learning
  • 3D scene generation using cost-effective radar / multi-modal sensors
  • Dynamic scene composition for robot manipulation and interaction with deformable objects
  • Simultaneous sensor and traffic simulation for novel environment adaptation in robotics
  • 3D Vision-Language dataset generation for robotic action learning and task execution
  • Deploying autonomous robotic system with data-driven closed-loop simulation evaluation
  • Efficient on-robot neural scene representation for real-time decision-making
  • General-purpose robot learning trained with a large corpus of synthetic data
  • ...

📨 Submission



Submissions should follow IROS format (i.e. IEEE conference template) and not exceed 4 pages including figures and tables but excluding references and potential supplementary material. The submitted abstracts will be peer-reviewed in IROS format (single-blind). Accepted abstracts will be made non-archival public through this website, and a portion of the accepted papers will be orally presented at the workshop. Any type of contributions, either already published (or accepted) or works in progress, are warmly welcome.
